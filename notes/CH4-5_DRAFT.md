# Chapter 4 — Auramaxxing
**Theme: the illusion of hierarchy — and the betrayal.** You reach the top, and the same day you learn the throne makes enemies of the people who put you near it. The mentor becomes the villain.

## Scene 0 — Getting there *(transition beat — don't drop cold into the party)*
> **Narrator:** Friday. All week the school's buzzed about one thing — Brayden's party. Open invite, which has always meant everyone except you.
> **Player (internal):** (Old me stays home and scrolls.) (...New me?)
> **Narrator:** You don't get invited. You just show up. That's the whole statement.

## Scene 1 — Arrival (the glow)
`[MUSIC: low, confident bass — felt more than heard]` `[transition: night — a house thumping at the end of the street]`

Brayden's place. Lit up, overflowing, bass rattling the windows. You walk up the drive like you own the deed. Brain, Frame, Aura — all trained. It shows.

> **Narrator:** The bass is loud enough to feel in your jaw. The jaw you've been working on.
> **Narrator:** Heads turn. Not the way they used to. Not to look away.
> **Player (internal):** (Don't smile. Let them look.)

## Scene 2 — The double-take *(replaces the cut Stacy scene)*
Validation comes from the apex flinching, not from a compliment.

> **Narrator:** Brayden sees you across the room. For half a second his face forgets to be amused.
> **Brayden:** ...the hell is *he* doing here?
> **Narrator:** He recovers fast. But the kid beside him caught it. So did the two behind them.
> **Narrator:** *The room felt the apex flinch. The room never misses that.*

## Scene 3 — Eugene plant *(small, easily missed — sets up Ch5)*
> **Narrator:** By the drinks table, someone's restocking cups. Unpaid, probably. Folded-in shoulders. You'd know that posture anywhere — you used to have it.
> **Eugene:** (not looking up) Open bar's not really open. But the ice is free.
> **Narrator:** Eugene. He works everywhere. Nobody ever sees him.
> *(Whether you saw him as a person back in the bunker is already locked from Ch3 — no choice here, just a glance.)*

## Scene 4 — The roast → THE MOG-OFF → the finisher
Brayden, status threatened, tries to chop you publicly.

> **Brayden:** YO. Everybody. Look who crawled out. The LTN. Used to eat trash out of his own backpack. Say something, chopped boy.

`[MECHANIC: the Mog-Off — culmination of all training. Three quick rounds, each countering a Brayden move with a trained skill. (Optional decorative "pick your move" per round; outcome is fixed — you win.)]`

- **Round 1 — BRAIN** (Ch2 callback): he tries to out-talk you; you bury him in vocabulary. He blinks.
- **Round 2 — FRAME** (Ch3 callback): posture/jaw standoff; he breaks first.
- **Round 3 — AURA** (this chapter): pure presence; the room is already leaning your way.

**Brayden's low blow** — cornered and losing, he goes for the oldest wound:

> **Brayden:** At least I don't eat WET SANDWICHES out of my own backpack, you —
> **Narrator:** And you laugh. Not a comeback. A real, surprised-out-of-you laugh — because it's *funny*, and because it doesn't touch you anymore.
> **Player:** (easy) Man. That sandwich was so bad.

**The finisher — the silent nod.** No dialogue wheel. You don't argue; arguing implies the outcome's in question.

> `[SFX: bass-boosted nod hit — deep, room-silencing]`
> **Narrator:** You just... nod. Once. Slow. The room reads it instantly. So does Brayden.

`[ART: "MOGGED" letters slam across Brayden's face]` `[SFX: GTA-V mission-failed sting]`

> **Narrator:** Brayden's mouth is still open. Nothing's coming out. He leaves — not storms, *leaves* — fast, not looking back.

## Scene 5 — The coronation begins
`[MUSIC: warm, rising — the "you made it" theme]`

The room turns to you. The whole party. Word travels before you've even left.

> **Narrator:** This is the part where you used to disappear. Instead, the room opens up around you like you've always belonged at the center of it.
> **Narrator:** By the time school comes back around, it's not a rumor anymore. It's a fact. There's a new name at the top.

The crown is *being placed.* The high point. *(Hold the MOGKAGE title card — it pays off in Ch5 once the crown is actually earned, not just handed over.)*

## Scene 6 — Clav's turn: the betrayal *(villain reveal — Ch4 closer)*
Clav finds you, riding the high. He does **not** say "well done."

> **Clav:** That's the most aura this school has seen in a decade. So take Brayden's spot. *Become* the thing they were all scared of. That's the whole point.
> **Player (internal):** (Become the new bully. ...Is that really the point?)
> **Narrator:** You don't bite. And something in his face changes — like he just watched the one thing he can't control walk away from him.

`[The turn: Clav got *close* to the ceiling (Ch3, "I got close"). You walked past it like it was nothing. He can't carry that.]`

That night the whispers start. And they're *precise* — they hit the exact insecurities Clav spent three chapters helping you bury.

> **Narrator:** "Heard the glow-up's fake. Heard he still cries about a sandwich. Heard someone *made* him — and he's nothing without the guy who did."
> **Narrator:** You know that voice in the rumor. You'd know it anywhere.
> **Narrator:** *The man who built you knows exactly where the cracks are.*

`[SFX: phone buzz — a dozen at once]`

> **Player (internal):** (...Clav.)

`[ART/BEAT: hard cut to black on the realization — the crown still in the air, already poisoned.]`

**END OF CHAPTER 4 — crowned and betrayed, same day.**

# Chapter 5 — The Final Boss
**Theme: the reckoning & the consequences.** A full chapter to face what your mentor became — and to decide what *you* become. The first choice in the whole game that truly matters.

## ACT 1 — The Poisoned Crown *(buildup)*
`[MUSIC: the warm coronation theme, now curdled — same motif, wrong key]`

Monday. The crown is yours on paper — but Clav's rumors are doing their work. Eyes slide off you again. The aura you earned is being talked out from under you.

> **Narrator:** You can feel it in the hallway. The lean-away. The thing you spent every chapter clawing out of.
> **Narrator:** Half the school thinks you're a fraud. The other half is waiting to see which way it breaks.
> **Narrator:** At the edge of the cafeteria, half-hidden — Eugene. Watching you, not the noise.
> *(Eugene plant #2 — make sure the player clocks him before the endings.)*

You trace the lies to their source. Of course you do. It was never hidden — he wanted you to know.

## ACT 2 — The Reckoning
A massive circle forms in the cafeteria. This time it isn't Brayden in the middle.

> **Clav:** There he is. The king. You know a crown's just a target, right? I should know. I wore one.
> **Clav:** I built you. Every word, every rep. And you used it to walk *past* me.
> **Clav:** So I'm going to take it all back. Right here. In front of everyone.

`[TENTATIVE — the planted mirror line (see bible's Bully lens):]`
> **Clav:** You think you're the first kid I found at the bottom? *I ate that sandwich too.* I just never found anyone willing to pull me up. So I stopped believing anyone gets pulled up.

### The apocalyptic Mog-Off
`[MUSIC: the real final-boss track]` `[ART: screen goes white-hot, stats flaring]`

The one true Mog Battle. Mentor vs. student, everything on the line. And then — the first choice the game has ever played straight:

> **THE FINAL CHOICE:**
> - **Mog him back.** (Pure, concentrated toxicity.)
> - **Refuse to mog.**

*(Everything before this was a fake choice. This one is real — the game spent four chapters earning that distinction.)*

## ENDING A — The Dark Chad *(bad ending)*

**The Action.** You out-mog Clav with every cruel, superior instinct he trained into you. He shrinks, stammers, drops to a sniveling normie at your feet.

> **Narrator:** You win. Of course you win. He taught you how. And it feels *incredible.* That's the problem.

**The Crime.** Drunk on aura, you walk out and a cop asks for ID. You won't break your mewing streak to answer.

> **Police Officer:** Sir. ID. Now.
> **Player:** (jaw clamped) ...*mmmnh.*
> **Player (internal):** (Jawline wind-resistance training. He wouldn't understand.) `[SFX: handcuffs]`

**The Courtroom.** The judge has perfect, *relaxed* posture. Effortless. He adjusts his cuff — a **$25 Casio calculator watch** underneath.

> **Narrator:** He doesn't look up. Not once. `[SFX: tiny rubber buttons — tick, tick, tick]`
> **Narrator:** He punches the numbers in on the watch. He is *calculating* you. Like arithmetic.
> `[ART: your stats — INT / FRAME / AURA — crack, then shatter like glass]`
> **Judge:** (still not looking up) Thirty days. Community service.

**Result:** GAME OVER. *Utility mogs aura.* You became all surface, no substance — and you became Clav. The cycle just got a new king.

**Eugene's color (from the Ch3 choice):**
- **Mogged Eugene (③):** as your stats shatter, *re-show the bunker closet and replay his line — "...you're one of them now."* The arrest is the bill for a choice you made a chapter ago. Earned, not arbitrary.
- **Lifted Eugene (④, the tragic one):** during the Clav fight you caught Eugene's face in the crowd — not afraid, just disappointed. You *proved* you could be better, then chose this. The wound is that you knew the other road.

## ENDING B — The True Gigachad *(good ending)*

**The Action.** You reject the hierarchy entirely. You don't mog Clav. You turn — not on him, but *to the room.*

> **Clav:** ...what are you doing. Hit me. Finish it. That's what this IS.
> **Player:** No. I'm done climbing on people.

**The Payoff — the "Power of Friendship" beam.** You use maxed-out stats for the one thing Clav never taught: lifting instead of mogging. You go around the cafeteria and sincerely, specifically *see* people.

> **Player:** Your essay last week was incredible. — You've got the best laugh in this school and you hide it. — You. Stand up straight. You're taller than you think.
> **Narrator:** One by one, the room stops bracing for a fight. The rumors have nothing to hold onto.

**The Eugene gate (the Ch3 choice cashes in):**
- **Lifted Eugene (①, full good):** the room's still half-skeptical — until **Eugene steps forward first.**
  > **Eugene:** He spotted me when nobody else would. Back when he had nothing to gain from it. He didn't have to.
  > **Narrator:** It lands *because* it's the lowest-status person in the room saying it. The crowd swings — all the way.
- **Mogged Eugene (②, lonelier good):** same noble choice, but **Eugene stands arms-crossed and says nothing.** He remembers the closet. The crowd hesitates; the one person who'd know your character withholds it. You still win them — but *alone,* the hard way. *(Tone: earned solitude, not the game scolding you.)* On your way out, Eugene gives one small, ambiguous nod. Not forgiveness. A door left open.

**Clav's exit.**
> **Narrator:** There's nothing here for him to fight. `[ART: Clav dissolves into blinding wholesome light — vampire-at-dawn]`
> **Narrator:** He doesn't lose a battle. He just stops being relevant. And then he's gone.

`[ART: NOW the title card lands — MOGKAGE. The crown was never about being feared. It's earned here, the right way.]`

**Final scene — the steps.**
`[MUSIC: the Gigachad theme — in a major, uplifting key]`

You sit on the school steps. Beside you: **Eugene** (if lifted), and a humbled **Brayden** — no longer the apex, just a guy.

> **Brayden:** ...so. no hard feelings about the, uh. sandwich thing.
> **Player:** It really was a bad sandwich.
> **Narrator:** And for the first time, the bottom of the food chain and the top of it are just... sitting on the same step.

`[CUT TO BLACK]`

> **It was never about becoming a Chad.**
> **It was about the friends you mogged along the way.**

**END.**
